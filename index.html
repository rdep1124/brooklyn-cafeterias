Today’s Lunch Special is Poorly Maintained Kitchens: Brooklyn’s Fight for Clean School Cafeterias

When 11% of the total health code violations reported in recent years happen in one Brooklyn area, what are children supposed to do to prevent ingestion of poorly kept food?

By: Ray DePaul

Do you know what’s in your child’s school lunch?

Data shows, if they go to school in Brooklyn, it’s probably festering with health code violations. These reports have only grown in recent years.

As per New York law, health code violations in New York City schools are public information. When reporters investigated it, they found a high concentration of around 11% of the violations originate from one community board, #312, in Brooklyn.

Why does this specific area in Brooklyn fail to meet health code standards with its varying types of schools, both public and private?
